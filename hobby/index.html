Home

Powerlifting is a strength sport that involves lifting the maximum weight possible for three different exercises: squat, bench press, and deadlift. The goal is to achieve the highest total weight lifted across all three exercises, with each lift judged by judges to ensure proper form and technique.

I enjoy powerlifting because it challenges me both physically and mentally. It pushes me to see what I am capable of achieving and helps me to stay disciplined and focused. My long-term goal is to achieve a 1500lbs total, which would require significant dedication and hard work. I am excited to continue pushing myself and to learn more about this amazing sport.

Training Schedule

My powerlifting training involves 5 days a week in the gym. Four of those days are intense and challenge me both physically and mentally, while the fifth day is an easier session to maintain blood flow in my muscles.

My coach provides me with a weekly training plan based on my goals for meet day and my performance over the past few weeks. He prescribes different variations of the squat, bench press, and deadlift, along with varying rep ranges, weight ranges, and the number of sets to complete for each exercise on a given day. His knowledge and experience help him to create these customized plans.

At the end of each week, we discuss my progress and address any weak points for the following week.

Weekly Training Schedule

  • Monday: Squat, then bench press, then accessories
  • Tuesday: Deadlift, then bench press, then accessories
  • Wednesday: Accessory exercises
  • Thursday: Squat, then accessories
  • Friday: Deadlift, then bench press, then accessories
  • Saturday and Sunday: Rest days

I prefer to lift for longer sessions on fewer days per week, rather than lifting heavy six times a week for shorter workouts. Accessories are exercises that are less intensive but help to improve the strength of the muscles required for squatting, bench pressing, and deadlifting.

How We Train

The conjugate method is a popular strength training program that involves rotating through different exercises over a period of time. This allows athletes to focus on different muscle groups and develop a well-rounded level of strength. The program typically includes both max effort work (lifting heavy weights for low reps) and dynamic effort work (lifting lighter weights for higher reps at a faster pace).

One aspect of the conjugate method is the use of banded and chain work for speed. Bands and chains are used to add resistance to the barbell at the top of the movement where the athlete is strongest. This allows them to work on explosiveness and speed through the entire range of motion. As the bar moves away from the athlete, the resistance from the bands or chains decreases, making it easier to complete the lift. The goal is to use a weight that is challenging but allows the athlete to maintain proper form and perform the lift with speed and explosiveness.
